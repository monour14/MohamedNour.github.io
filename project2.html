Shark Attacks in Egypt

Data Visualization Geographical Distribution Historical Data

Project Purpose

The core objective of this project was to investigate the frequency, lethality, and geographical distribution of shark incidents in Egypt from the 1890s to the 2020s.

Key Insights & Findings

Through comprehensive data visualization, the analysis reveals that attacks are historically rare (only 44 recorded events). The dashboard highlights that 84.1% of these incidents were concentrated in the Red Sea. Furthermore, the data identifies the Oceanic Whitetip as the most commonly involved species in fatal incidents.
